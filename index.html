Wright Wildlife Systems

Precision-designed wildlife collars engineered to survive

In 1902, the year before their first successful flight, the Wright brothers were dismissed as dreamers. Two bicycle mechanics, told that human flight was impossible, proved the world wrong through persistence, clarity of thought, and a refusal to accept the limits of the day.

At Wright Wildlife Systems, that same spirit drives us. Wildlife researchers have spent decades relying on GPS collars that fail, break, or disappear when they’re needed most. The status quo has been tolerated for far too long.

We take a different path.

Guided by the principle of via negativa — removing everything unnecessary until only what works remains — we design tracking systems stripped down to their essential purpose: stay online, stay attached, and come home at the end of their life.

Our collars are built for the largest, most aggressive animals on the continent. Every component is chosen for species-specific durability. Every system uses redundant, independent electronics. Every design is tested with the same stubborn determination that once lifted a wooden glider into the air.

Our mission is simple:
Build wildlife tracking devices that do not fail.
Build collars that can be recovered.
Build tools researchers can trust with the animals they protect.

Wright Wildlife Systems exists to outperform, outlast, and out-deliver anything that came before — because wildlife deserves better, and so do the people studying it.

Collar Features

General Features

  • A single, robust GPS and VHF housing – no wires running through the collar.
  • Highly accurate GPS and reliable Iridium & Globalstar transmission, even when housing is under the neck.
  • Completely independent VHF system with its own batteries and PCB – unmatched reliability.
  • Dual GPS and Iridium antennas for stronger, faster communication.
  • Heavy-duty belting that is 4× stronger than competitors.
  • Ergonomic bolt placement at the top of the collar for better fit and no breakage.
  • One-size-fits-all design – only need the average neck size.

Iridium Collars (Powered by Xeos Technologies)

  • High-voltage system – superior performance and cold-weather reliability.
  • True 2-way communication – adjust GPS/Iridium schedules instantly without manufacturer contact.
  • Optional VHF integration.
  • Higher temporal resolution for precise tracking.
  • 5-year lifespan.

Globalstar Collars (Powered by GeoForce)

  • Dual redundant devices – always one device facing skyward.
  • Optional single-device configuration.
  • Optional VHF integration.
  • Reports location every 12 hours per device.
  • Lightweight, simple design with a 5-year lifespan.
